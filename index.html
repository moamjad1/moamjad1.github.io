LinkUp — UX Case Study

Problem

College students often struggle to build meaningful social connections due to time constraints, social anxiety, and uncertainty around others’ availability.

POV

Busy, socially disconnected college students want low-pressure, structured ways to interact, so that they can connect without the fear of feeling awkward or risking social rejection without spending time and effort they do not have.

My Role

I contributed to the design and evaluation of the LinkUp interface, focusing on improving the search functionality to make finding and adding friends more intuitive.

Process

We conducted user research, developed low-fidelity prototypes, and iterated into high-fidelity designs based on usability testing. Early designs had confusing navigation and unclear interactions, which we simplified through testing.

Design Decisions

  • Introduced swipe-based discovery
  • Used bottom navigation for clarity
  • Structured forms to reduce friction

Usability Issues

We identified issues such as unclear visibility of hangout requests and overlapping UI elements, and improved hierarchy and interaction cues based on Nielsen’s heuristics.

Evaluation

We evaluated efficiency, effectiveness, and user impact to measure how well the interface supported real-world interactions.

Reflection

We learned the importance of early user involvement and challenging assumptions about how users manage their schedules.
